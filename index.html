Roof Replacement FAQs

Answers to common questions about estimating roofing replacement labor rates, materials selection, and hiring conditions.

How much does a new roof replacement cost on average in 2026?

On average, replacing a typical 2,000 square foot roof in the United States costs between $7,500 and $16,500.

Basic architectural asphalt shingles represent the lowest pricing entry point, whereas premium architectural shingles, standing-seam metal roofs, and natural slate range significantly higher (upward of $25,000–$45,000 depending on pitches and sizing metrics).

How are material and labor costs calculated in online roofing estimates?

Estimates are calculated using three independent cost vectors:

  • Materials: Priced per "Squares" (1 square = 100 sq.ft of surface area) plus structural underlayment, flashing, and vent caps.
  • Labor: Priced according to complexity. Steep pitched roofs or multi-story structures incur heights premium labor multipliers.
  • Removal: Demolition of old roof structures is roughly $1.25–$2.50 per square foot depending on single or multi-layer shingles setups.
